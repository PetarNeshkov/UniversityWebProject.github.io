How to smoke a cigar?

True to the principle "If you enjoy, then you do it right.", Here are the basic recommendations for smoking a cigar.

  • A cigar should be smoked slowly and with pleasure.
  • It should not be inhaled, as is the case with other tobacco products.
  • It is drawn in carefully and the smoke is left in the mouth to feel the rich flavors through the palate.
  • One should be relaxed and try to preserve the feeling of the mix of flavors and aromas of the blend.
  • If the cigar goes out, it is not a problem to resume it with a new kindling, as the residual ash must be removed first.
  • The cigar can be smoked with pleasure up to at least ¾ of its length.
  • Ash and what will happen to it should not be a cause for concern.
  • When smoking a cigar, it is not customary to shake the ashes like a cigarette, but to let them fall into the ashtray alone.
  • After the tasting, the cigar is not "extinguished" in the ashtray, but is left to burn with dignity by itself.

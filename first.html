How to cut a cigar? What are the types of incisions?

Wondering how to cut a cigar?


The cut of a cigar should be made just above the line where the lid meets the cover sheet (for figural cigars about 3 mm below the top). The goal is clear - to make a hole wide enough to ensure unobstructed suction, and at the same time not to cut too much, because the cigar will begin to develop.

What tools can you use to cut a cigar?


There are several tools that can be used to cut a cigar.

Here they are:

  • The most popular tool is the guillotine cutter - single or double blade.
  • The alternative is a scissors cigar cutter.
  • Another option is a "punch" - a cutter with a round blade that removes part of the cap that protects the shape of the "head".
  • V-shaped cutters are suitable for figured cigars. The lid should not be pierced with a matchstick or cocktail stick. This will cause the composite sheets to compact and prevent suction.

..and now .. enjoy your cigar!
